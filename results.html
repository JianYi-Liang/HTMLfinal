1. What colors can carrots come in?
      2. Answer: All of the above
    • Carrots are not only orange, but can come in multiple colors, such as red, orange, yellow, purple, and white.

  2. What family is the carrot apart of?
      3. Answer: Apiaceae
    • The carrot is apart of the Apiaceae family, along with celery, fennel, parsley, and parsnip.

  3. Which state produces the most carrots in the USA?
      4. Answer: California
    • California is the top state in the U.S. that produces carrots, producing 80% of the county's carrots.

  4. True or False: Eating too much carrots can make your skin turn orange
      5. Answer: True
    • Carrots are rich in beta-carotene, which is what gives the carrot its high levels of vitamin A and orange color. Having too much beta-carotene in your bloodstream can cause you to develop carotenemia, which can turn your skin orange.

  5. What vitamins can carrots give you?
      6. Answer: Vitamin A, Vitamin K1, and Vitamin B6
    • While carrots have an abundance of vitamin A, vitamin K1, and vitamin B6, carrots do not have any vitamin D, which is most commonly found in animal-based and fortified foods, as well as the sun.
